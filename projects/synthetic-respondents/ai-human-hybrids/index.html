AI-Human Hybrids

Revolutionizing Marketing Research with a Large Language Model

The Stakes

Marketing research is expensive, slow, and increasingly hard to run

The scale

Companies spend billions annually on consumer panels — qual IDIs, quant surveys — before any product launches.

The quality problem

Human panels suffer from acquiescence bias, positivity bias, and satisficing. Even expensive studies are unreliable.

The access problem

Hard-to-reach respondents — executives, niche B2B buyers — are slow and very expensive to recruit.

The question: can LLMs assist at multiple stages of the marketing research process — and how well?

The Framework (Table 1)

LLMs can assist at every stage — not just data collection

The paper maps LLM roles across the full research process.

Study Design: Generate discussion guides, draft survey questionnaires, create A/B concepts.

Sample Selection: Identify respondent characteristics. Generate synthetic personas.

Data Collection: Synthetic respondents for IDIs and surveys.

Data Analysis: Theme extraction, summarization, statistical visualization.

The human supervisor remains essential throughout — especially for problem definition and final judgment.

The Empirical Design

Two real studies from a Fortune 500 food company — replicated with GPT-4

Study 1 · Qualitative

Friendsgiving messaging research. Originally conducted in 2019.

In-depth interviews (IDIs). Multiple LLM hybrid conditions tested against human-only benchmark.

Study 2 · Quantitative

Refrigerated dog food concept test. n=605 real respondents (2019).

Likert-scale survey. Three synthetic conditions (zero-shot, few-shot, RAG) vs. human benchmark.

The original human studies serve as ground truth. The LLM is benchmarked against them directly.

Part 1 of 2

Qualitative Research

Study 1: Where in the IDI process can LLMs add value — and how much?

Study 1 · Design (Table 4)

Not one hybrid — four. Each assigns a different task to the LLM.

The discussion guide is always human-led. Everything else can vary.

LLM Hybrid 1 — Synthetic Respondents: Mirror original human profiles exactly. LLM plays the respondent.

LLM Hybrid 2 — Recruitment: LLM also selects who to interview. Generates novel personas beyond original demographics.

LLM Hybrid 3 — Moderation: LLM acts as respondent AND moderator. Scores each answer; probes when score <80/100.

LLM Hybrid 4 — Moderation + Recruitment: Full LLM control of everything except the discussion guide.

Study 1 · LLM Hybrid 3

The LLM moderator scores every answer and probes when depth is low

Four scoring dimensions per answer-turn:

Relevance

Is it on-topic?

Depth

Is it comprehensive?

Clarity

Is it detailed?

Informativeness

Is it new and useful?

Average score <80/100 → LLM generates a follow-up probe. Score ≥80 → next question.

Example: initial answer scores 71.25 → probe issued → re-answer scores 83.75 → proceeds.

Hybrid 3 produces the highest information density and coherence of all hybrid conditions.

And it is semantically closest to human-generated data (BERT embeddings).

Study 1 · Results (Tables 7 & 9)

LLM responses are deeper and more informative — but harder to read

Depth score

+0.68

LLM responses score significantly higher on depth (p<.05) — more detail-oriented.

Informativeness score

+0.50

More informative (p<.05). Human evaluators rate LLM answers as more novel and useful.

Readability

12+

Years of education needed to read LLM responses. Human responses: 5th-grade level. LLM is verbose and complex.

Clarity and Relevance: no significant difference between LLM and human responses.

Theme recovery: Human–LLM hybrid recovers 96% of themes AND discovers 23% new ones humans missed.

LLM-only: 77% recovery. The best hybrid is always better than either alone.

Study 1 · Expert Judgment (Table 10)

Expert judges unanimously preferred hybrid summaries over pure human or pure LLM

3 of 5 judges preferred:

Human Generated — LLM Analyzed

"Good examples, right amount of detail and storytelling."

2 of 5 judges preferred:

LLM Generated — Human Analyzed

"Thorough and interesting. Both functional and emotional lens."

Zero out of five judges chose the all-human or all-LLM summary as best.

Hybrid wins regardless of whether it's the data or the analysis that's LLM-assisted.

Bonus: LLM Hybrid 2 (recruitment) identified niche personas humans missed — vegans, LGBTQ+, restaurant chefs with Friendsgiving menus.

Part 2 of 2

Quantitative Research

Study 2: Can synthetic respondents replicate real Likert-scale survey data?

Study 2 · Setup

605 real dog owners. 605 synthetic personas. Same demographics.

The 2019 dog food survey is the ground truth. Three LLM conditions compete against it.

LLM1 — Zero-shot: Demographics only in system role. No other context.

LLM2 — Few-shot (Response History): Previous answers for this persona are fed back in-context before the next question.

LLM3 — Few-shot + RAG: Response history plus transcripts from a prior qualitative study on pet food (16 respondents) as external knowledge.

Three metrics compared: Mean bias · Heterogeneity · Internal consistency (correlations)

Lower = closer to human benchmark.

Study 2 · Results (Table 13)

Mean bias stays stuck. Heterogeneity and correlations get dramatically better.

Mean bias: ~0.66–0.69 across all three models. Context does not fix this.

LLMs are about 0.7 points off (on a 1–5 scale) regardless of prompting strategy.

Heterogeneity: LLM1=0.41 → LLM2=0.29 → LLM3=0.28. Both few-shot conditions significantly better (p<.05). LLM2 and LLM3 tie.

Correlations: LLM1=0.40 → LLM2=0.27 → LLM3=0.11. RAG delivers the largest gain here (p<.05).

Each level of context adds something. RAG is the key to recovering the correlation structure between survey items.

Study 2 · The Diagnosis

Why does zero-shot collapse variance? Demographics explain almost nothing.

The zero-shot system role is built almost entirely from demographics — age, gender, income, region, education.

But demographics explain only a tiny fraction of consumer attitudes and behaviors. The LLM doesn't know what makes each person unique beyond those labels.

Result

Every persona gives a similar answer. Synthetic responses cluster around the same point on the scale.

Standard deviation is significantly lower than human data (p<.05).

The fix

Give the LLM context about how this particular persona has responded before (few-shot), or domain knowledge from qualitative research (RAG).

The LLM isn't wrong — it's just underspecified. More context = more realistic heterogeneity.

Practical Takeaways

What this means for synthetic panel research

Safe to use zero-shot for

Direction-checking. "Does this product score low or high?" The LLM is reliable here.

Do NOT use zero-shot for

Variance analysis, segment comparisons, conjoint/choice models, or anything that depends on the distribution shape or inter-variable correlations.

Add few-shot (response history)

Significantly improves heterogeneity and internal consistency. Low cost — just pass prior answers back in-context.

Add RAG for correlations

If you have prior qual data on this category, use it. RAG halves the correlation error (0.40 → 0.11). Critical for psychometric validity.

For qualitative: LLM as moderator (Hybrid 3) is the strongest condition. Always keep human judgment for final synthesis.

The Upshot

The hybrid is not a shortcut. It is a better process.

"LLMs and humans bring unique, complementary insights to the table — the future of marketing research should involve a structured collaboration between them."
