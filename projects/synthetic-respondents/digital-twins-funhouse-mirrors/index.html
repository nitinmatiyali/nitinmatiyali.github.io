Paper #8 · Peng, Gui, Toubia et al. · Columbia · 2026

Digital Twins as
Funhouse Mirrors

Five Key Distortions in LLM-Based Human Simulations

19 pre-registered studies. 164 outcomes. 1,784 real people matched to their digital twins.
The verdict: twins look human — but systematically distort what it means to be one.

The Promise

Digital Twins Sound Revolutionary

A digital twin: an LLM loaded with everything known about a specific person — asked to answer as them. The pitch: surveys without recruiting people, experiments without ethical constraints, panels at zero cost.

Startups valued at ~$1B have been built on this premise. Fortune 500 companies are deploying it today.

But has anyone rigorously tested whether digital twins actually work?

The Problem

Prior Evidence Was Narrow

Encouraging results from early studies reported 72–88% relative accuracy.

But those studies used known, previously published experimental tasks — likely already in the LLM's training data — and measured group-average accuracy, not whether each twin correctly matched its specific human.

This is the first study to test twins on novel questions, across many domains, with individual-level matching.

The Mega-Study

The Biggest Twin Test Ever

19 pre-registered sub-studies spanning Creativity, Politics, Privacy, Labor Market, Fairness, Behavioral Economics, News Consumption, and Technology Platforms.

164 distinct outcomes matched human-to-twin at the individual level — across 1,784 unique U.S. participants, a representative sample, not convenience.

Twin Construction

The "Full Persona" Approach

Each human answered 500+ questions in an extended 145-minute multi-session survey.

Blueprint data

14 demographic variables

279 personality questions (26 constructs)

85 cognitive ability items

82 economic prefs & heuristics

Method

All ~128,000 characters of profile data loaded into GPT-4.1's memory at once.

Then: answer this new question as this person.

This is the best possible digital twin built with today's technology.

The Result

Weak Correlation. Modest Accuracy.

Average correlation between twin and human responses across 164 outcomes:

r = 0.20

On a scale from 0 (no match at all) to 1.0 (perfect match), 0.20 is weak — roughly the correlation between height and intelligence.

The Damning Gap

145 Minutes of Data. Zero Extra Accuracy.

Full persona accuracy: 0.748

Demographics only accuracy: 0.746

That 0.002 difference is statistically indistinguishable from chance (p = 0.37 — a high p-value means no meaningful difference was detected).

Loading in 500+ personality and cognition questions barely moves the needle over just knowing someone's age, race, income, and politics.

The twin is mostly using demographic stereotypes — not individual cognition.

Five Distortions

Why Twins Fail

Performance is modest overall. But the paper goes further — it identifies five systematic ways digital twins distort human behavior.

① Stereotyping

② Insufficient Individuation

③ Representation Bias

④ Ideological Biases

⑤ Hyper-Rationality

Distortion 1

Stereotyping

Twins over-rely on demographics, ignoring the rich individual-level data. The test: how different are full-persona twins from demographics-only twins?

Mean Absolute Deviation (MAD): full twin vs. demographics-only = 0.132
Mean Absolute Deviation (MAD): full twin vs. real human = 0.252

MAD = the average gap between two sets of answers. A value of 0.252 means the twin's answers were, on average, a quarter of the rating scale away from the real person's.

Twins are closer to their demographic clone than to the real human they're supposed to represent.

Distortion 2

Insufficient Individuation

Twins are too well-behaved. Real humans are widely dispersed in their views.

Twin standard deviation (SD — how spread out responses are) < Human SD in 154 of 164 outcomes (93.9%).

On average, twins are 37% less spread out than real humans.

Think of the base model as having strong built-in default assumptions. The persona data should override them — but it doesn't. Full-persona twins end up closer to the empty-persona base model than to their real humans.

Distortion 3

Representation Bias

Twin accuracy is not uniform across the population. The model favors people already over-represented in training data.

In practice: brands using digital twins to simulate lower-income or politically extreme consumers — often the hardest audiences to reach through traditional research — will get the least reliable results.

Better accuracy for

Social science already over-studies WEIRD populations (Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic). Digital twins will systematically amplify this bias.

Distortion 4

Ideological Biases

Twins don't just fail to match human opinions — they substitute their own. The biases originate in how the model was trained — the text it learned from and the human feedback used to shape its responses.

"Pro-Human" lean

More trusting of other people

More willing to pay healthcare taxes

More pro-regulation of fees

"Pro-Technology" lean

More accepting of algorithmic hiring

Less concerned about online privacy

Under-reporting Netflix / TikTok use

Dark twist: twins scored human-generated ideas lower than AI-generated ones when rating creativity. For teams using twins to test creative concepts, this means results could systematically favor AI work — a material conflict of interest.

Distortion 5

Hyper-Rationality

Humans are irrational. We fall for defaults, decoys, compromises. Twins don't.

Twins failed to replicate the attraction effect (adding an inferior option makes another look better by comparison) and the compromise effect (people prefer the middle option between two extremes) — classic signs that humans use mental shortcuts, not pure logic.

Twins displayed perfect factual knowledge on questions humans got wrong.

Exception: on well-known published paradigms, twins did show the effect — but only because they memorized the published result from training data. In other words, the twin passed the test by having read the answer key, not by genuinely simulating a human. This is leakage, not fidelity.

The Hidden Problem

Prior "Success" May Be Leakage

When a twin replicates a known effect, it might just be recalling the published finding from training data.

Classic (known) paradigm

Twin does show the expected effect.

Trained on the paper that proved it exists.

Novel paradigm

Twin does not show the expected effect.

No training signal to copy from.

Studies that validated digital twins using known paradigms may have measured memorization, not simulation.

Calibration

What's a Digital Twin Actually Worth?

The authors compare twins to XGBoost (a standard machine learning model trained on real human responses, used as a benchmark) — asking: how many real respondents does it take for the trained model to match twin performance?

~180

real respondents for XGBoost to match full-persona twin correlation

<0.29

max XGBoost correlation even with 650 real respondents

Twins ≈ running your study on ~200 real people. Useful — but far from replacing human research.

The Verdict

Funhouse Mirrors, Not Windows

Digital twins are not faithful replicas. They are systematic distortions that look deceptively human.

They create an illusion of personalization — but underneath, they mostly encode demographic stereotypes and LLM priors.

Deploying them at scale risks producing confident, plausible-looking, systematically wrong conclusions.

The technology isn't worthless. But it needs honest benchmarking — on novel paradigms, at the individual level — before use in consequential research or policy.

Summary

The Five Distortions

① Stereotyping

Over-relies on demographics, ignores individual cognition

② Individuation

Responses cluster near the base model — too homogeneous

③ Representation

More accurate for WEIRD populations — amplifies existing bias

④ Ideological

Pro-human, pro-technology, human-averse in creativity tasks

⑤ Hyper-Rationality

Too smart, too consistent — fails to replicate how real humans use mental shortcuts and make imperfect, shortcut-driven decisions
